The advent of autonomous vehicles had heralded a transformative era in transportation, reshaping the landscape of mobility through cutting-edge technologies. Central to this evolution was the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and learning algorithms, propelling vehicles into realms of unprecedented autonomy. This paper provided a comprehensive exploration of the evolutionary trajectory of AI within autonomous vehicles, tracing the journey from foundational principles to the most recent advancements. Commencing with a current landscape overview, the paper delved into the fundamental role of AI in shaping the autonomous decision-making capabilities of vehicles. It elucidated the steps involved in the AI-powered development life cycle in vehicles, addressing ethical considerations and bias in AI-driven software development for autonomous vehicles. The study presented statistical insights into the usage and types of AI/learning algorithms over the years, showcasing the evolving research landscape within the automotive industry. Furthermore, the paper highlighted the pivotal role of parameters in refining algorithms for both trucks and cars, facilitating vehicles to adapt, learn, and improve performance over time. It concluded by outlining different levels of autonomy, elucidating the nuanced usage of AI and learning algorithms, and automating key tasks at each level. Additionally, the document discussed the variation in software package sizes across different autonomy levels.
